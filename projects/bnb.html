Ball and Beam Platform

Design, mechanical development, and dynamic modelling of a portable mechatronic control platform

Project Details

Completed: June 2026

This project focused on the complete design, modelling, construction, and experimental validation of a compact Ball and Beam platform intended for automatic control education. Rather than purchasing a commercial system, the objective was to develop a fully integrated mechatronic platform that combines custom mechanical design, embedded electronics, sensing, and mathematical modelling into a portable laboratory setup.

The project emphasized the complete engineering workflow, from conceptual design and CAD modelling to manufacturing, subsystem integration, and validation of the dynamic model. The resulting prototype provides a robust experimental platform suitable for future controller development while remaining affordable and easily reproducible.

  • Custom mechanical transmission using an eccentric disk mechanism
  • Portable 35 × 40 cm platform integrating mechanics and electronics
  • Custom PCB for sensing and actuator interfacing
  • Lagrangian-based mathematical modelling
  • Experimental model validation using the physical prototype

Mechanical Design

The mechanical architecture was designed around three primary objectives: portability, repeatability, and ease of fabrication. Unlike many educational Ball and Beam systems that rely on multi-link lever mechanisms, this design employs an eccentric disk transmission that converts motor rotation directly into beam motion while minimizing backlash and reducing the number of mechanical interfaces.

The transmission uses a belt-and-pulley reduction to improve angular resolution and torque, while adjustable bearing supports simplify assembly and alignment. The complete assembly was developed in CAD before fabrication, allowing interference verification and dimensional validation prior to manufacturing.




Manufacturing & Integration

The prototype combines multiple manufacturing technologies to balance cost, mechanical performance, and ease of replication. Laser-cut acrylic provides a smooth rolling surface for the beam, while structural supports, bearing housings, and transmission components were manufactured using PETG additive manufacturing. Commercial bearings, pulleys, and shafts were integrated with custom machined parts to produce a rigid and serviceable platform.

A custom PCB integrates the infrared position sensor, motor driver, encoder interface, and Raspberry Pi Pico microcontroller, creating a compact self-contained experimental platform suitable for classroom and laboratory environments.

Dynamic Modelling & Validation

A complete mathematical model of the system was derived using Lagrangian mechanics, describing the relationship between beam angle and ball position while accounting for the rolling dynamics of the sphere. Unlike classical educational models, the project also incorporates experimentally identified actuator dynamics, resulting in an extended third-order representation that more accurately captures the behavior of the physical system.

Experimental testing demonstrated that including actuator dynamics dramatically improves agreement between simulation and reality, producing a model that closely reproduces the measured transient response. This validated model provides a reliable foundation for future control strategies, system identification, and educational experiments.
